YOU CAN'T WIN A COMPARISON IF YOU'RE BEING COMPARED ON THE WRONG CRITERIA

Two operators competed for the same mountain cabin listing last spring. Same market. Similar track records. Comparable commission structure.

One walked in with a revenue projection, an occupancy comparison, and a management fee breakdown. The other opened the conversation differently: "Before I tell you about us, can I share one observation from working this specific area that most owners I talk to don't know yet?"

The second operator got the property. Not because their numbers were better. Because they changed what the owner was evaluating before the evaluation started.

This is Jill Konrath's insight-led conversation framework from Agile Selling applied to the listing appointment.

THE FEATURE TRAP

The feature-led pitch is the default. Most operators talk about what they do, how their team is structured, what software they use, how many properties they manage, what their review scores look like. These are features. They invite comparison. When you lead with features, you've agreed to be evaluated on the same terms as everyone else in the room.

The insight-led opener changes the game. It introduces a criterion the owner wasn't using before. If the criterion matters, the owner reorganizes their evaluation around it. And you introduced it.

WHAT AN INSIGHT OPENER SOUNDS LIKE

For a self-managing owner who has been running their own listing:

"Most owners I work with in this area think the biggest revenue opportunity is pricing optimization. In our experience, the variable that predicts long-term revenue most reliably is review velocity in the first 90 days. Properties that accumulate 15-20 reviews in the first 90 days perform 30-40% better over 24 months than similar properties that accumulate them slowly. Here's what that looks like for your specific market..."

That's an insight. It's specific. It's counter to what most owners assume. It introduces a metric they weren't tracking. And it positions everything you say next as coming from an operator who understands their situation more precisely than the other two managers they're meeting with this week.

Field note: Investor owners are the most receptive to insight-led openers because they're already thinking in metrics. The yield comparison, the occupancy gap, the review velocity curve: these map onto how Investors think. Steward owners respond to insights framed around property care and guest experience quality rather than revenue metrics. Know which insight to lead with before you walk in the door.
