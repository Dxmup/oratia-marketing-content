The owner who still thinks self-management is more profitable than professional management, or that the national firm's scale gives them an attention advantage, isn't ready to hear your pitch. They need context first.

April Dunford's positioning framework requires you to establish the market landscape before you describe your service. The shift in OTA algorithms. The direct booking revenue gap. The math of what 60 to 80 properties per portfolio manager actually produces in terms of attention per property. These aren't arguments against your competition. They're explanations of why the owner's current experience feels the way it does.

The newsletter this week covers how to build that context-first structure from your specific market and operator profile, how to deliver the competitive de-positioning without sounding dismissive, and how to run every capability in your current pitch through a translation filter. If you can't complete the sentence "which means for you that..." in concrete, specific terms, the feature isn't ready to be in the proposal. The capability-to-value translation table is in the newsletter, and it covers the six features VRM operators most often present in the wrong language.
