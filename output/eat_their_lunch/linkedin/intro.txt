Most of the owner acquisition conversations we have in VRM are not net-new. The owner is already with someone else, self-managing, or locked into a contract they've been quietly dissatisfied with for a year.

Iannarino's Eat Their Lunch is the framework I keep coming back to when I see it. Not because it's a VRM book. Because it's the most precise map I've found for what's actually happening when you're trying to win business from a competitor.

This week I'm running three posts applying it to owner acquisition. If you work in VRM business development, these are worth your time.
