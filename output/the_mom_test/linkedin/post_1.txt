THE QUESTION YOU'RE ASKING IS THE PROBLEM

When you ask an owner "Are you happy with your current manager?" you are not doing discovery. You are giving them permission to be polite.

The answer you get back: "mostly, yeah, there's always room for improvement." That tells you nothing. You have learned that the owner doesn't want conflict. That's it.

Rob Fitzpatrick's Mom Test is built around a simple observation: if you ask your mom whether your business idea is good, she'll say yes because she loves you. Not because it's true. Owners do the same thing in listing appointments. They are not trying to mislead you. They just don't want to be rude to a stranger sitting in their living room.

The fix is not to probe harder. The fix is to ask better questions.

HYPOTHETICAL VS. HISTORICAL

"Would you like better maintenance communication?" Every owner says yes. It's a meaningless signal.

"What happened the last time there was a maintenance issue at the property?" That question produces real information.

One owner describes a missing doorknob that took three weeks to fix. Another describes a guest complaint about a broken AC in July that the manager didn't return calls on for two days, and the owner drove 45 minutes to handle it themselves at 9 PM. Those are different owners with different pain depths. The first may not be ready to switch. The second is.

You cannot tell the difference if you ask hypothetical questions. Hypothetical questions produce hypothetical answers. Past-behavior questions produce real diagnosis.

THE QUESTION AUDIT

Before your next listing appointment, write down the three questions you most want to ask. Then ask yourself: does this question allow the owner to give me negative information?

"Are you happy with your current situation?" No.
"What would better management look like to you?" No.
"What was the last thing that frustrated you about how this property is managed?" Yes.

The phrasing is the diagnosis. If your questions are designed to generate agreement, you will get agreement. You will also walk out thinking the appointment went well when the owner was already planning to stay put.
