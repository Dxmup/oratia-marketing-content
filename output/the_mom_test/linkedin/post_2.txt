THE OWNER WHO LOVED YOUR PLATFORM ISN'T GOING TO SIGN

Owners who say "this is exactly what I've been looking for" and ask zero questions about transition, fees, or existing bookings are not your best prospects. They are your most polite ones.

There is a version of a listing appointment that feels like a win. The owner is warm. They're enthusiastic about the platform demo. They say things like "wow, this is impressive" and "I've never seen anything like this." You leave feeling confident about where they sit in your pipeline.

That owner is often simultaneously deciding to stay with their current manager.

WHAT ENTHUSIASM ACTUALLY SIGNALS

Compliments are social lubricant. Fitzpatrick makes the argument that buyers who intend to buy ask hard questions. They want to know about the transition process. They want to understand fee structures. They worry about guest reviews during the changeover. They ask what happens if something goes wrong in the first 90 days.

Polite non-buyers give you compliments because they don't want to tell you no in the room.

The most reliable signal in a listing appointment is not enthusiasm. It is friction. Skeptical questions. Requests to slow down. "What happens if I'm not happy with how you handle a guest complaint?" is the question an engaged owner asks. "This all looks great" is the question an owner who is planning to wrap up the meeting asks.

WHAT TO DO WHEN AN OWNER IS UNUSUALLY WARM

Probe deeper, not less.

Script: "I appreciate that. Before we talk next steps, I want to make sure I understand your situation fully. What concerns do you have that we haven't addressed?"

That question tests whether the enthusiasm is real or social. The owner who has genuine intent will name a concern. The one who is being polite will struggle to name one, which tells you something different: they're not yet at the point where the decision is real.

Pipeline quality is not about warmth. It is about committed information. An owner who has told you their actual concern is further along than one who has complimented your software three times.
