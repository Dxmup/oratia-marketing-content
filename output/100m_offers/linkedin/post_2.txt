THE DREAM OUTCOME IS DIFFERENT FOR EVERY OWNER TYPE

Many VRM operators build one pitch and deliver it to every room. "Maximize your ROI," "protect your asset," "stress-free management" gets repeated regardless of who's sitting across from them. The problem: Hormozi's Dream Outcome variable is not a universal statement. It's a personal one. It varies by owner type, and getting it wrong costs the appointment.

THE SIX DREAM OUTCOMES IN VRM

THE INVESTOR: A property producing return at or above their benchmark, with predictable cash flow and minimal time overhead. "We'll maximize your ROI" lands. What lands harder is a specific three-scenario forecast and telling them which one you'd bet on.

THE STEWARD: Their property cared for properly, guests having genuine experiences in a space that means something to them. "We treat the home like our own" is the industry's most overused line. What a Steward needs to hear is that you understand why the home matters before you mention a number.

THE SKEPTIC: A management relationship that delivers what was promised, with no surprises and no feeling of being abandoned after signing. The Skeptic's dream outcome isn't exceptional performance. It's a manager who doesn't disappear. Frame your offer around proof of follow-through.

THE DELEGATOR: Complete operational removal from the equation. Not "mostly hands-off." Not "we handle the day-to-day." Actually not thinking about it. Every element of your offer should signal total operational separation.

THE CONTROLLER: A partner who executes inside clearly understood boundaries and keeps them informed without requiring them to ask. The Controller doesn't want to give up control. They want a partner who respects the parameters.

THE OPPORTUNIST: A property that pays for itself and requires less of them than they feared. The Opportunist's dream outcome isn't aggressive. It's relief.

Script: "Before I walk you through what we do, I want to understand what success looks like for you specifically. For some owners it's about maximizing revenue. For others it's about protecting the property. For others it's about being completely out of the operational picture. Which of those is closest to what you're actually solving for?"

Field note: The Delegator and Opportunist are the two owner types most likely to disengage if the first five minutes are revenue-heavy. Both are solving for simplicity, not optimization. Open with questions about their current involvement before you introduce any performance data.
