OVER-PROMISING IS FUTURE CHURN

Disappointment is the gap between expectation and reality. That gap is set the day the owner signs.

Many VRM operators win contracts by promising maximum potential revenue. They look at a best-case pro forma, ignore market volatility, and sell the dream. This happens systemically because the BD rep is incentivized to close today, while operations and revenue management live with the consequences tomorrow.

THE PSYCHOLOGY OF DISAPPOINTMENT

If you promise an owner $100K in gross revenue and deliver $90K, you've failed. They feel cheated and start looking for a new manager.

If you promise $80K and deliver $90K, you're a hero. They become an evangelist for your brand.

The financial outcome was identical. The psychological outcome was completely different. The only thing that changed was the expectation set at signing.

THE THREE-SCENARIO FORECAST

Elite VRMs build around conservative transparency. Stop giving owners a single projection. Give them three:

- Pessimistic: What happens if the market softens 15%, a competing property opens next door, and their home underperforms the comp set on reviews. This is the floor.
- Realistic: Median outcome based on comparable properties in their zip code, trailing 12 months. This is the number you commit to.
- Optimistic: What's possible if conditions break favorably. Present this last, without anchoring to it.

Walk through the pessimistic case first. Show them exactly what would have to go wrong to get there. Then show the realistic case and anchor your commitment to the middle, not the top.

Script: "Many managers will show you the upside and ask you to sign. I'm going to show you three scenarios and commit to the middle one. If we beat it, you'll be thrilled. If we fall short, we'll have a specific conversation about exactly why. I'd rather set a number I can beat than sell you a projection that turns into an argument six months from now."

A contract won on a fantasy is future churn. A contract won on a conservative truth is the foundation of a long-term portfolio.

Field note: The three-scenario model plays well with Investors, who think in ranges and risk-adjusted returns. With Opportunists, the pessimistic scenario can create cold feet. Be ready to walk them through why the realistic case is genuinely realistic, not just middle-of-the-road hedging.
