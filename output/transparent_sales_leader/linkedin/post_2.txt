YOUR PIPELINE IS A LIE

Stop tracking what your sales team does. Start tracking what the homeowner does.

Many VRMs measure their acquisition pipeline based on BD team activity. Emails sent. Meetings held. Contracts delivered. This isn't a forecast: it's a wish list.

A contract sent means nothing if the homeowner hasn't engaged with it. A meeting held means nothing if the owner left with no intention of moving forward. Todd Caponi's definition of real forecasting is verifiable buyer actions, not seller activities.

THE SYSTEMIC DELUSION

When a VRM tracks "contract sent" as a 70% probability of closing, they're lying to themselves. The homeowner hasn't committed to anything. They're holding a piece of paper. Meanwhile, the owner is experiencing cognitive load: reviewing fees, evaluating risk, deciding whether they trust you. Your BD rep's activity metric ignores that psychological reality entirely.

VERIFIABLE BUYER ACTIONS

Rebuild your pipeline stages around what the owner does, not what your team does. Real advancement signals:

- Owner shares their trailing 12 months of revenue or booking data unprompted.
- Owner introduces their spouse or financial partner into the conversation.
- Owner asks about the termination clause without being prompted.
- Owner provides a specific target go-live date.
- Owner forwards your proposal to their CPA or attorney.
- Owner asks how to notify their current manager.

A simple pipeline built on buyer actions:
- Stage 1: Interest (owner agreed to a discovery call)
- Stage 2: Qualified (owner shared property or revenue data)
- Stage 3: Engaged (owner introduced a partner or asked a transition question)
- Stage 4: Committed (owner named a go-live date or started the cancellation conversation with their current manager)
- Stage 5: Signed

If the owner hasn't taken a verifiable step, the deal is stalled, regardless of how many follow-up emails your CRM shows.

Track the owner's movement, not your team's noise.
