The owner who spends the whole listing appointment managing their resistance isn't difficult. Their brain is doing exactly what brains do when they detect that someone wants something from them.

Phil Jones's framework isn't a collection of manipulation tricks. It's a vocabulary engineered to keep the Zone of Resistance from fully activating. When you open with "I'm not sure if we're even the right fit for your situation," you're not being self-deprecating. You're signaling low pressure, which is the only thing that allows the owner's brain to actually process what you're saying rather than filter it for threat.

The newsletter this week maps the full Jones language system to the VRM appointment arc: how to open without triggering defensiveness, how to bridge from small talk to substantive conversation, how to introduce a disruptive insight without sounding like a critic, and how to move toward a decision without applying the kind of pressure that freezes owners at the finish line. The irony of the Jones approach is that by removing pressure at each stage, you move through the stages faster.
