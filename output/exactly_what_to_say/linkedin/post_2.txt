THE JONES LANGUAGE PATTERNS MAPPED TO THE FULL VRM APPOINTMENT

The Zone of Resistance doesn't activate once at the door and stay quiet. It reactivates at every transition: when you introduce a new topic, when you bring up the fee, when you move toward a close. Jones's micro-scripts give you language for each of those moments.

THE DISCOVERY BRIDGE

Goal: move into the substantive conversation without triggering the defensive shift that comes from "now I'm going to show you what I'd do."

Script: "How open-minded are you to a different approach to the way this property is currently positioned?"

This asks for permission to introduce a new frame before introducing it. The owner who answers "pretty open-minded" has given themselves psychological permission to hear something that challenges their current thinking.

THE FEE CONVERSATION OPENER

Script: "Just imagine for a moment what it would feel like to have this property running at its potential without any of the daily management falling on you."

Jones calls this future-pacing. When the owner can feel the outcome, the fee becomes the price of the feeling rather than the cost of a service. Those are different purchasing decisions.

THE COMMITMENT TEST

Script: "If everything we've discussed today made sense, what would the next step look like for you?"

A commitment test dressed as a logistics question. The owner who says "I'd want to talk to my spouse" is telling you where the friction lives.

THE CLOSE

Script: "Would it make sense to get you on our onboarding calendar for [month] and start the paperwork this week?"

"Make sense" frames the decision as logical. A no to "does this make sense?" feels less final than a no to "are you ready to sign?" Same decision. Different pressure.

THE FOLLOW-UP WHEN THEY WANT TO THINK

Script: "Of course. What specifically would be most useful to think through? I want to make sure you have what you need."

This treats "I need to think about it" as an information gap. Owners with a specific unasked question are more likely to surface it when invited.

THE DESIGN PRINCIPLE

Every Jones pattern operates on the same logic: give the owner's brain a way to say yes to the next step without feeling committed to something irreversible. Less pressure at each transition means faster decisions.

Field note: The Jones patterns are most effective when internalized, not delivered verbatim. The principle is that every transition needs a permission-seeking opener. Generate language that feels natural to how you actually talk.
