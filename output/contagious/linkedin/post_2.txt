THE OWNER WHO PASSED ON YOU SIX MONTHS AGO IS PROBABLY READY NOW

Most VRM operators lose deals not to a better competitor but to timing.

The owner wasn't ready in January. They had just renewed with their current manager, or they weren't frustrated enough yet, or the switch felt like too much friction at the wrong moment in the year.

Then something shifts. A bad summer. A maintenance nightmare. A fee increase from their current company. And suddenly the decision they weren't ready to make in January becomes the one they're actively thinking about in August.

If you're not already in their mental rotation, you don't get the call.

Jonah Berger calls this Triggers in Contagious. The principle is simple: top of mind drives tip of tongue. People act on ideas that are present when the moment arrives, not ideas they heard once and have to work to remember.

THE ACQUISITION TIMING PROBLEM

Most VRM operators pitch when they're ready. Not when the owner is ready. These are almost never the same moment.

The listing appointment in January is not the decision point. The decision point is the first weekend in August when a guest complaint hits at 2 AM and the current manager is unreachable.

You need to be somewhere in the owner's field of vision when that happens.

HOW TO BUILD TRIGGERS WITHOUT SPAMMING

The operators who consistently show up at the right moment are not the ones running the most aggressive outreach. They're the ones who built simple, reliable presence.

A useful market update that lands in inboxes the first week of every month. Not a pitch. A number. A trend. Something an owner would actually read.

A brief check-in timed around peak season, framed around their specific property category, not a generic "just checking in."

Content that surfaces at the moments owners are most likely to be frustrated with their current situation. Posting consistently is not vanity. It's a timing strategy.

Field note: The Skeptic owner responds poorly to repeated outreach. For that owner type, a content touchpoint is far less threatening than a call, and it does the same job. Let the useful information do the work.
