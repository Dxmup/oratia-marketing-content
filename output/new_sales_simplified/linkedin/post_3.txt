IF YOU CAN'T NAME THE PROPERTIES YOU'RE GOING AFTER, YOU DON'T HAVE A PROSPECTING STRATEGY

"Doing outreach" is not prospecting. Prospecting starts with a specific list.

Mike Weinberg is blunt about this in New Sales. Simplified.: elite new business developers know exactly which accounts they want. They've built a list. They work the list. The best salespeople don't prospect at a market. They prospect at a named set of specific targets.

Too many VRM operators approach owner acquisition the opposite way. They put up a website, get listed on directories, and wait for owners to find them. That's visibility. It's not prospecting.

THE LIST

A real target list has specific properties on it. Not "owners in our market" as a category. Actual properties you have identified as high-probability targets.

Building that list:

1. Define your ideal property profile. Bedroom count, location, likely owner type, current platform, revenue potential. A two-to-three bedroom standalone in your highest-performing neighborhoods, currently self-managed, owner who lives out of market. That's a profile.

2. Identify properties that match. Use Airbnb and Vrbo filtered for target neighborhoods. You're looking for properties that match your profile and aren't already with a professional manager you respect.

3. Build a named list of 30-50 targets. Each with a contact field, status field, and next-action field. This is your prospecting pipeline.

4. Work it on a schedule. One outreach attempt per target per two weeks. Track responses. Adjust based on what's working.

This is operationally different from "we're doing outreach." The list makes prospecting measurable. Without a list, you can't tell an active pipeline from a slow one.

Field note: Oratia tracks owner acquisition by property target, not general market activity. The shift from "we're doing outreach" to "we are working a named list of 42 target properties" is a meaningful change. It makes acquisition results predictable, which is not something most operators can say.
