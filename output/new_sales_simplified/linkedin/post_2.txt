THE ACQUISITION SLUMP YOU'RE IN RIGHT NOW? YOU BUILT IT 90 DAYS AGO.

VRM operators don't have owner acquisition slumps. They have pipeline discipline failures that show up 90 days later.

The pattern is almost always the same. Spring is strong, new properties are signing, the revenue projections look good. Then summer arrives. The phone rings constantly. Guests have problems. Owners have requests. Cleaners go missing on turnover days. Every hour is already spoken for.

Prospecting stops. Not intentionally. It just gets displaced.

Then October arrives. The operational load lightens. And the pipeline is empty.

Mike Weinberg's core argument in New Sales. Simplified. is that sales slumps are almost never caused by what's happening in the market right now. They're the visible result of what stopped happening three months ago. The operator who attributes their dry fall to "slow season" or "market conditions" is usually looking at the wrong cause.

THE DISCIPLINE FLOOR

Weinberg's fix is simple and uncomfortable: define a non-negotiable minimum and protect it.

Not a goal. A floor. The minimum prospecting activity you commit to regardless of how busy operations gets. If you normally do ten owner outreach contacts per week, the floor might be three. Not zero.

The floor keeps the pipeline moving even when it isn't moving fast. And it prevents the full collapse that creates the boom-bust cycle most operators recognize and few diagnose correctly.

In practical VRM terms, this looks like:

- Two new outreach attempts per week minimum, even during peak season
- One follow-up sequence check-in, even if it's just a quick email
- One recorded note from any listing appointment, even if informal

These are small commitments. What they prevent is large: the October moment where you realize the only warm conversation you have is one owner who has been "thinking about it" since February.

Field note: The Controller owner type takes the longest to convert. Many operators walk away from them too early when they're busy with easier wins. The discipline floor keeps you in their pipeline over the 12-18 months it sometimes takes. Giving up during a busy season often means losing deals that were one or two follow-ups from closing.

The slump is built quietly, one displaced prospecting hour at a time.
