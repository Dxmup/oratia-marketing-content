THE WINDOW CLOSES FAST

There's a short period when a property owner is genuinely open to a management conversation. Most operators miss it.

Jill Konrath calls these trigger events: the moments when something disrupts an owner's status quo enough that switching stops feeling risky and starts feeling necessary. The timing of your outreach matters more than the quality of your pitch.

WHAT TRIGGERS LOOK LIKE IN VRM

Not all of them are obvious. Some are:

A second or third 3-star review appearing on their listing in the same month. An owner who just got a maintenance call at 2 AM and handled it themselves. A short-term rental regulation change in their jurisdiction that just made self-management more complicated. A neighbor's property that just got professionally managed and is now visibly outperforming. A guest damage claim that cost the owner more than they expected and reminded them there's no one in their corner.

These aren't rare events. They happen in every market. The operator who has a system for recognizing them and reaching out within days is not competing on pitch quality. They're competing on timing.

THE OUTREACH PROBLEM

Many VRM operators run outreach on a spray-and-hope schedule. Monthly emails to a cold list. Social posts hoping the right owner sees them. The occasional introduction through a referral network.

That approach finds owners who happen to be ready. It misses the owners who would have been ready last Tuesday, when they were sitting on hold with Airbnb support for 45 minutes trying to mediate a damage dispute.

At Oratia, we've seen this pattern across operator pipelines: owners who engaged within a week of a trigger event converted at meaningfully higher rates than owners who came in through general outreach. The difference isn't who they are. It's when they were reached.

BUILDING A TRIGGER SYSTEM

It doesn't have to be complicated:

Set Google Alerts for short-term rental regulation news in your target markets. Monitor review scores for properties you're tracking. Stay active in local host groups where owners surface frustrations before they reach out to anyone. Ask your signed owners what finally prompted them to call.

Field note: Opportunist owners move fast when a trigger fires, but they also move on fast if you don't follow up. The operator who responds within 48 hours of a trigger event is in a different sales conversation than the one who reaches out two weeks later with a polished deck.

Script: "I saw your listing got a regulatory question in the county last week. A few of my owners in that same area have been navigating that. Would it be worth 15 minutes to talk through how they're handling it?"

That's not a pitch. It's a timely, relevant reason to start a conversation. Right now, that's more valuable than any pitch you could run.
