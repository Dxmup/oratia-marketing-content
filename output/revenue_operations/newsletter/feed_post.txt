When VRM operators analyze churn, they focus on the triggering event: the maintenance complaint, the bad review, the owner who said they wanted to try something different. The failure almost always began in the 90 days before signing.

The revenue projection the BD rep presented and the number the property manager believes they're working toward are often two different numbers. The communication cadence described in the listing appointment and the cadence the PM believes the owner expects are often two different things. Both gaps are manufactured during the sales process and surface as operations problems 6 to 18 months later.

The newsletter this week covers how to audit those gaps, the 90-day onboarding protocol that closes them before they become churn events, and how to build the Ideal Owner Profile as an operational specification rather than a marketing persona. There's also a four-question handoff audit you can run right now against your last five new contracts. The number of operators who discover a gap in those four answers is not a small number.

The newsletter covers all of it.
