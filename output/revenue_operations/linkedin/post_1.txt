YOUR OWNER CHURN ISN'T AN OPERATIONS PROBLEM. IT'S A SALES PROBLEM.

The most expensive assumption in vacation rental management is that owners leave because of bad cleaning.

When an owner churns, many operators blame operations: the maintenance team, the housekeepers, the pricing engine. Sometimes those things contributed. But the failure was usually set in motion months earlier, during the sales process.

THE HANDOFF IS WHERE TRUST DIES

Stephen Diorio's central argument in Revenue Operations is that sales, marketing, and customer success aren't separate departments. They're a single continuous revenue system. In VRM, we build silos instead.

The BD rep sells a reality: total peace of mind, elite revenue, perfect care. The owner is then handed to a property manager who lives in a different reality: broken HVACs, difficult guests, tight margins, a 200-item task list. The owner doesn't churn because the HVAC broke. They churn because the BD rep said they'd never have to think about the HVAC, and then the property manager sent a bill with no context and no relationship.

The owner doesn't feel disappointed. They feel duped. Duped owners don't just leave. They tell their neighbors.

THE FIRST 90 DAYS ARE STILL THE SALE

The gap between what was promised at signing and what the owner experiences in the first 90 days is what Diorio calls a revenue leak. In VRM, that leak is almost always worst during onboarding, when the owner forms their real impression of the company they actually bought.

The fix isn't better operations. It's treating onboarding as an extension of the sale.

THE THREE-PART HANDOFF FIX

1. Stop letting BD sell things operations can't consistently deliver. Audit the promises against operational reality before any contract is signed.

2. Build a shared data model. The property manager inheriting a new owner must see every projection, every promise, and every expectation the BD rep set. Different information means a structural trust gap.

3. Run a calibration call at onboarding. Script: "I want to make sure we're aligned on what the next 90 days looks like. Here's what you should expect from us, and I'd like to know what was described during your decision process so we can deliver against it."

Field note: If your BD rep and your property manager can't independently write down the same top three priorities for a new owner, you have a commercial operating system problem. The CRM isn't a sales tool. It's the single source of truth for every promise made to every owner in your portfolio.
