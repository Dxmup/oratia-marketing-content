MOST PROPERTY OWNERS LIE ABOUT WHY THEY WON'T SIGN

If you solve the objection they give you, you'll often lose the deal.

When a homeowner tells you they want to "think about it" or "try managing it themselves for a summer," they are almost never telling you the actual reason. These are reflex responses, deployed to avoid the social discomfort of a direct no and to buy time from decision-making. The systemic failure of most VRM sales processes is taking them at face value.

THE THREE COMMON SMOKESCREENS

"I want to try it myself first." Usually means: I'm not sure I trust you enough to hand over control yet.

"Your fee is too high." Usually means: I'm not convinced the outcome will justify the cost.

"I need to talk to my spouse." Sometimes true. Often means: I need an escape from making this decision today.

If you counter a self-management objection with a time-savings spreadsheet, you're having a logical argument with an emotional defense mechanism. The defense mechanism doesn't care about your spreadsheet.

ISOLATING THE GHOST

You can't overcome an objection you haven't identified. The technique is to acknowledge the smokescreen without fighting it, then remove it to expose the real friction underneath.

Script: "I completely understand wanting to try it yourself. Outside of wanting to see how the Airbnb side works for a season, is there anything else that's holding you back from making a decision today?"

That phrase, "outside of" the stated objection, conceptually removes the smokescreen and forces the owner's brain to look behind it. They will often reveal the actual barrier.

"Well, honestly, I'm just really worried about who's going to be in my house. I want to be involved in vetting the guests."

Now you're not arguing about commission versus self-management economics. You're in a specific conversation about risk and guest vetting protocols, a conversation you can actually win, with specifics about your screening standards, your damage protection, your occupancy rules.

Stop fighting the smokescreen. Isolate the fear. Solve the root problem.
