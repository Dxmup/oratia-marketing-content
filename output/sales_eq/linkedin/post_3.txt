THE PLATINUM RULE IN OWNER ACQUISITION

Stop treating every homeowner the same way.

Many VRMs build one master listing presentation and deliver it to everyone. The same pitch to the retired couple looking for supplemental income as to the aggressive real estate investor looking to maximize yield. It consistently loses deals that should close.

THE GOLDEN RULE IS THE WRONG RULE

The Golden Rule says treat others the way you want to be treated. In sales, that's dangerous. It assumes the prospect processes information the way you do. The Platinum Rule: treat others the way they want to be treated.

THE SIX SIGNALS IN THE FIRST FIVE MINUTES

The most useful single diagnostic: how does the owner refer to the property?

"The unit" or "the asset": almost always an Investor or Delegator frame. They're evaluating an investment. Lead with comps and yield math.

"The lake house," "my grandfather's cabin," "our place": almost always a Steward or Controller frame. They're evaluating a custodian. Lead with appreciation for the property and specificity about care.

"I haven't decided whether I'm actually going to list it yet": Opportunist. They need to feel educated before they feel committed. Sell the concept before you sell the service.

"The last manager really dropped the ball": Skeptic. They've been burned. The Accusation Audit is your opening move, not a revenue projection.

"How much can I make and what do you charge?" in the first two minutes: Driver. Get to the point and don't slow down for relationship-building they didn't ask for.

ADAPTIVE OPENINGS BY OWNER TYPE

Script (Investor): "Let me start with comps. I pulled trailing 12 months for three properties within a quarter mile. Here's what the data says."

Script (Steward): "Before we talk about anything else, tell me about the property. What matters most to you when guests are here?"

Script (Skeptic): "I'm going to guess the last manager promised more than they delivered. Before I make any claims, tell me exactly what went wrong, so I can tell you whether we're actually different or just saying we are."

Script (Delegator): "Here's what this looks like from your end: we onboard you in 30 days, honor any existing bookings, and you get a monthly report and a check. Let me walk you through the three decisions you actually need to make."

Script (Opportunist): "There's no pressure here to sign anything today. What I'd love to do is walk through the numbers on your specific property and let you decide whether the math even makes sense for your situation."

The appointment you thought went well but didn't close was usually an owner-type mismatch. You delivered the right content to the wrong person. Read the owner before you run the pitch.
