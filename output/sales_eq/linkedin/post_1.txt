OWNERS DON'T CARE ABOUT YOUR REVPAR. NOT YET.

Lead with spreadsheets and you've often already lost the pitch.

Many VRM operators walk into owner presentations armed with dynamic pricing charts, occupancy projections, and comp set analyses. They assume the owner is making a rational, mathematical decision. They often aren't. Not first.

THE EMOTIONAL BRAIN DECIDES FIRST

Sales EQ is built on a core finding from behavioral science: humans make decisions emotionally and justify them with logic afterward. In VRM acquisition, this dynamic is amplified because you're not selling software or a service contract. You're asking for the keys to a deeply personal, highly valuable asset.

When a homeowner sits across from you, their cognitive load is consumed by perceived threats:
- Will my home get destroyed by a bad guest?
- Will the neighbors complain to the HOA?
- Will I get frantic calls at 2 AM?
- Will the cleaners take something?
- Will strangers judge how my family uses the space?

When you open with RevPAR and pricing algorithms, you're answering questions they haven't asked yet. Their actual emotional state goes completely unaddressed.

THE SEQUENCE FIX

Solve for peace of mind first. Then solve for revenue.

Script: "Before I show you any numbers, I want to acknowledge something. Many owners at your stage are less worried about revenue than about three specific things: damage, neighbor complaints, and middle-of-the-night emergencies. Not because revenue doesn't matter, it does, but because those three things are the downside, and the downside is what keeps people up at night. Let me walk you through exactly what we've built to handle each one. Then we'll get to the math."

The owner almost always responds positively, because you've named the conversation happening in their head, not the one in your pitch deck.

Only after the emotional threat has been addressed does the logical brain fully engage with your pricing strategy. The math doesn't land until the fear has been answered.

Field note: Data Driver archetypes struggle with this most. If that's your natural style, build a 90-second emotional preamble into every appointment opening and rehearse it until it feels natural. Your data lands harder after you've made room for it.
