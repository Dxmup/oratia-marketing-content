A new owner who just recorded a deed is most receptive in the first 30 to 60 days before they've settled on a management approach. An owner whose review score just dropped is most receptive in the week the frustration is fresh. Timing is part of the relevance equation, and operators who don't have a system for knowing when those moments happen are relying on luck.

The TQ framework from Justin Michael and Tony Hughes is about building the intelligence layer that gets you in front of the right owners at the right moment, before your competitors who are sending generic outreach know the moment exists. Public property records, STR permit databases, review score monitoring, and MLS delisting data are all accessible to a small operator on a small budget. The question is whether anyone is actually tracking them.

The newsletter this week covers how to build a practical trigger event workflow, the three-touch outreach sequence that converts intelligence into appointments, and the neighborhood canvass protocol that turns every new property you sign into two or three additional prospect conversations on the same block within 72 hours.
