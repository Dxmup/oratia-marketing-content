WHAT A SPREADSHEET PIPELINE ACTUALLY COSTS

A spreadsheet doesn't forget follow-ups. People do.

When the follow-up lives in a spreadsheet, the rep has to remember to check it. Then remember what was said last time. Then remember what was promised. Then write a note after the call that may or may not be accurate six weeks later.

At 30 leads, this is manageable. At 80 leads, it breaks.

The spreadsheet didn't fail. The cognitive overhead of maintaining it manually, at scale, with everything else a VRM operator is running, exceeded what any person can sustain.

The operators who get past 100 properties didn't manage their pipeline better. They stopped relying on memory.
