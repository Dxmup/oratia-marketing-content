Most acquisition reps start the day the same way.

They open their CRM, look at 60 leads in no particular order, and spend 20 minutes deciding who to call. Some sort by last contact date. Some go alphabetically. Some just call whoever they remember from yesterday.

That's not a sales process. That's guessing.

Oratia opens to a single card: the lead that needs your attention right now. Name, persona type, last interaction summary, days since contact, and a suggested opener based on where the conversation left off.

The rep doesn't sort. They don't filter. They don't decide.

They read the card, pick up the phone, and start the call already knowing who they're talking to and why today is the right time to call.

The difference between a rep who makes 12 calls a day and one who makes 6 is rarely work ethic. It's how long it takes to start each one.
