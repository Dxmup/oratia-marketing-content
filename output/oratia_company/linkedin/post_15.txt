WHY GENERIC CRMS FAIL VRM OPERATORS

Generic CRMs are built for a different kind of sale.

In a standard B2B sales process, a "contact" is a professional at a company. The relationship is commercial from the first conversation. The product is a subscription.

In VRM acquisition, the "contact" is a homeowner. The asset they're considering handing over is often their most valuable possession, and in many cases, one they're emotionally attached to.

The fields don't map. The workflows don't translate. The language is wrong.

An "opportunity stage" that says "Proposal Sent" doesn't account for the owner who loved the appointment but is waiting for their spouse to finish a kitchen renovation before committing.

Oratia is built for the actual dynamics of the conversation, not a generic version of it.
