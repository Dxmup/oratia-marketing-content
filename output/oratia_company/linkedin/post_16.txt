THE NEW REP WHO STARTED FROM ZERO

A VRM company in Tennessee had a strong acquisition rep for three years.

He left in October.

His replacement started in November with a spreadsheet, a list of names, and no context on any of them.

She spent six weeks reconstructing the history of leads that already existed. Calling people who had said no six months ago. Missing the ones who were close to deciding.

The company lost a quarter of acquisition momentum, not because the new rep was bad, but because the pipeline knowledge walked out the door with the old one.

Oratia keeps the history in the system. Transitions don't start from zero.
