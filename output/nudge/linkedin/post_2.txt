YOUR DEFAULT PROPOSAL IS YOUR MOST LIKELY OUTCOME

Thaler and Sunstein documented the default effect across organ donation, retirement savings, and green energy enrollment. The finding is consistent: whatever is set as the default gets chosen at dramatically higher rates than alternatives, even when switching is easy.

The mechanism applies directly to VRM management proposals.

The default proposal is not a neutral starting point from which an owner negotiates. It is the most likely signed agreement. Owners do not optimize proposals. They anchor to what they are presented and evaluate from there.

THE PROBLEM WITH LEADING LOW

Many operators present a reduced offering as their standard: a shorter contract term to reduce perceived risk, a lower fee tier to seem accessible, a limited-scope package to get the door open. The reasoning is sound. The outcome often isn't.

When you set a 6-month trial as your standard offer, most owners will take it. Not because 12 months doesn't suit them. Because 6 months was the default.

When you set your lowest fee tier as the starting point, most owners will take it. When renewal or upsell time comes, moving them off the default requires them to actively switch, and people don't actively switch.

THE ALTERNATIVE

Present the arrangement you actually want as the default.

Not the version you think the owner will be comfortable with. The version that represents a full, healthy relationship: the right contract length, the right scope, the right fee.

If an owner needs to negotiate down from there, that is a legitimate conversation. But starting at the floor means there is nowhere to go but sideways.

Script: "Here's the arrangement we'd recommend for your property and your goals. Most of our owners are on this structure, and it's built around giving us the tools to actually deliver what we're promising."

That framing positions the default as the thoughtful expert recommendation, not a sales push. And it makes deviating from it feel like a choice the owner is making, not one being made for them.
