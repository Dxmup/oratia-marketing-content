OWNERS DON'T NEED MORE DATA. THEY NEED SOMEONE TO MAKE SENSE OF IT.

The most valuable person in a listing appointment isn't the one with the most data. It's the one who can make sense of the noise and help the owner understand what it means for their specific situation.

Many VRM operators arrive armed with spreadsheets, competitor analyses, and RevPAR projections. They assume the owner's primary frame is analytical. This is frequently a miscalculation.

Homeowners are already overwhelmed. AirDNA statistics. Neighbor gossip. Competing managers making conflicting claims. Online forums with wildly divergent advice. Throwing more data at them creates paralysis, not confidence.

THE SENSE-MAKER ROLE

Iannarino calls this the Sense-Maker function: the difference between a vendor presenting information and an advisor interpreting it.

When an owner asks about your commission rate, they're rarely asking about money. They're asking a deeper question: are you worth the risk of handing over my asset? When you respond by reciting a list of services to justify the fee, you've treated a trust question as a transactional one. You've commoditized yourself.

THE REFRAME

Contextualize what's actually happening in the market, explain the forces at play, and position your service as the intelligent response to conditions the owner didn't fully understand before they met you.

Script: "The market right now is overloaded with new inventory, and many managers are reacting by dropping rates to maintain occupancy. That strategy attracts lower-tier guests, accelerates wear-and-tear, and suppresses the review scores that drive long-term performance. Our approach goes the opposite direction: protect the asset first, optimize for guest quality second, and capture premium revenue through direct booking development and channel positioning. The commission conversation makes a lot more sense once you understand the alternative."

You've made sense of the market. You've moved from vendor to advisor. Now the owner isn't comparing your fee to a competitor's fee. They're evaluating whether your strategic orientation matches the outcome they actually want.

Different conversation. Different close rate.
