OWNERS DON'T NEED MORE DATA. THEY NEED SOMEONE TO MAKE SENSE OF IT.

Many VRM operators walk into listing appointments armed with spreadsheets, competitor analyses, and aggressive RevPAR projections. They assume the owner's primary frame is analytical. This is frequently a miscalculation.

Homeowners are already overwhelmed by the noise of the market. AirDNA statistics. Neighbor gossip. Competing managers making conflicting claims. Online forums with wildly divergent advice. Throwing more data at them creates paralysis, not confidence.

THE SENSE-MAKER ROLE

In a complex, noisy environment, the most valuable person in the room isn't the one with the most data. It's the one who can make sense of the noise and help the owner understand what it means for their specific situation. Iannarino calls this the Sense-Maker function, and it's the difference between a vendor presenting information and an advisor interpreting it.

When an owner asks about your commission rate, they're rarely asking about money. They're asking a deeper question: are you worth the risk of handing over my asset? When you respond by reciting a list of services to justify the fee, you've treated a trust question as a transactional one. You've commoditized yourself again.

THE REFRAME

Instead: contextualize what's actually happening in the market, explain the forces at play, and position your service as the intelligent response to conditions the owner didn't fully understand before they met you.

Script: "The market right now is overloaded with new inventory coming online, and a lot of managers are reacting by dropping rates across the board to maintain occupancy. That strategy attracts lower-tier guests, accelerates wear-and-tear on the property, and ultimately suppresses the review scores that drive long-term performance. Our approach goes the opposite direction: we protect the asset first, optimize for guest quality second, and capture premium revenue through direct booking development and channel positioning. The commission conversation makes a lot more sense once you understand the alternative."

You've made sense of the market. You've elevated from vendor to advisor. Now the owner isn't comparing your fee to a competitor's fee. They're evaluating whether your strategic orientation matches the outcome they actually want.

That's a completely different conversation. One that closes at a completely different rate.
