The owner who quotes an AirDNA projection as their expected return isn't being unreasonable. They just don't have the data you have. The question is whether you'll correct them.

Anthony Iannarino's One-Up framework reframes this as a responsibility, not a sales tactic. When you know things the owner doesn't, staying quiet in the interest of being agreeable isn't humility. It's a failure to lead. The VRM operator who can walk into a listing appointment and say "I want to make sure we're working from accurate numbers" before showing comp-set data the owner has never seen is doing something different from every other manager on their shortlist. Not more confident. More useful.

The newsletter this week covers how to build genuine information authority in your specific market, how to make the correction without sounding like you're grading the owner, and how to become the Sense-Maker in an environment where owners are being given wildly conflicting information about what their property is worth.
