THE OPERATORS SOLVING THE STATED PROBLEM ARE IN A PRICE COMPARISON

When an owner says "my reviews have been dropping," every manager they talk to promises better maintenance and higher cleaning standards. All solving the same stated problem. That is a feature comparison, and feature comparisons close on price.

Daniel Pink's argument: in a market where every owner can research your reviews and compare your fees before the appointment ends, problem-solving is commoditized. The rare skill is problem-finding: helping the owner see the real problem they couldn't articulate. The operator who does that competes on authority. Everyone else competes on price.

THE DIAGNOSTIC BEFORE THE PRESCRIPTION

Three common complaints and the diagnostic moves that surface the real problem:

Dropping reviews:

Script: "Before we talk about reviews, I want to understand what's upstream. A review score is a lagging indicator: it tells you something failed in the guest experience, but not where. Can you walk me through your current pre-arrival communication and post-stay follow-up? That's usually where review problems actually start."

Flat revenue:

Script: "Flat revenue is worth examining before we talk about what I'd do differently. If the comparable properties around yours are up 8 to 12 percent and you're flat, you're not holding steady. You're losing ground. I want to understand whether that's a pricing problem, a distribution problem, or something in the guest experience first."

Slow communication:

Script: "Before I tell you about our response protocols, I'm curious whether the communication gap is about information or just about speed. Are there surprises happening because things aren't being communicated, or are they being communicated too slowly? Those are different problems."

THE CLARITY REFRAME

Two questions that do this work in any listing appointment:

"If your maintenance response time were perfect, would that solve your main concern with this property?"

"If your occupancy doubled, would your current situation be sustainable, or would other things start to break?"

When the owner recalibrates their stated problem in real time, you are the operator who helped them see something no one else did.

Field note: The Investor's stated problem is almost always revenue. Their real problem is often a misalignment between their financial model and their manager's incentive structure. Surfacing that changes the close from "I'll get you higher ADR" to "I'll align my incentives with your actual goals."
