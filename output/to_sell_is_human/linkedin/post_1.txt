OPERATORS WHO LISTEN MORE CLOSE MORE

Many VRM operators walk into a listing appointment with a prepared presentation and leave without an agreement. They covered the technology, the reviews, the maintenance protocols, the fees. The owner said it was very helpful and they'd think about it.

That outcome is not a near-miss. It is the product of the wrong posture.

Daniel Pink's To Sell Is Human distinguishes empathy from attunement. Empathy is feeling what another person feels. Attunement is thinking like they think. The distinction sounds subtle. In a listing appointment, it is most of the game.

THE EMPATHY TRAP

Empathic response to an anxious owner: "I understand how stressful it must be dealing with a manager who isn't communicating."

Attuned response: "Walk me through what managing this property looks like right now. I want to understand your week before I describe mine."

The first validates the feeling. The second invites the owner to describe their full reality: what's wrong, what it costs them, what they've tried. That description is the material the close needs.

THE POWER REDUCTION MOVE

Pink's research found that reducing your perceived power in the room increases the probability of agreement. Not by making yourself smaller. By making the conversation less asymmetric.

Script: "I'm going to ask more questions than I answer, at least in the first half of this conversation. What I present at the end depends on what I hear from you first. The pitch I give a first-time owner looks different from the one I give an investor transitioning from a prior manager. Give me time to understand your situation before I tell you how we work."

THE REFLECTION BEFORE THE PITCH

Reflect back what you heard before presenting anything.

Script: "Before I respond, let me make sure I understood. Right now [their situation in two sentences]. The things costing you the most are [their pain points]. What you most need from whoever you work with next is [their stated priority]. Is that accurate?"

When the owner confirms, you've differentiated from every operator who opened with a deck. You haven't pitched yet.

Field note: The Skeptic tests in the first ten minutes: is this person listening, or waiting to pitch? The attuned opening answers that test before the real conversation starts. Operators who skip to the presentation fail it without knowing there was a test.
