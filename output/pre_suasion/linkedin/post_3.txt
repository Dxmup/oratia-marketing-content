OWNERS DON'T EVALUATE VRM OPERATORS ON YOUR BEST FEATURE. THEY EVALUATE ON YOUR FIRST FEATURE.

Two operators visit the same owner in the same week.

Operator A leads with their dynamic pricing engine, their OTA distribution, and their guest review management system. Strong pitch. All of it accurate.

Operator B leads with: "The thing I hear from owners who've been through a management transition is that the hardest part is not knowing what's happening with their property between the time they sign and the time they actually trust the system. We've built our entire communication process around fixing that."

After both appointments, the owner sits down with their spouse and compares the two options.

They compare them on owner communication.

Operator A has a communication process. It's good. But they never made it focal. Operator B made it the first thing the owner thought about. Now Operator B is competing on the dimension they chose. Operator A is competing on a dimension they never intended to compete on.

WHAT CIALDINI CALLS THIS

Cialdini's mechanism is called "what is focal is causal." What the mind is focused on in a decision is what appears to be the most important factor in that decision. The operator who controls what is focal first controls the evaluation framework.

This is not about deceiving anyone. The dimension you make focal should be one of your genuine strengths. The point is that you have to choose it deliberately and make it the first thing in the owner's mind, not leave it to chance.

HOW TO DECIDE WHAT TO MAKE FOCAL

Before each listing appointment, ask: on what single dimension do I most want this owner to evaluate my company?

Owner communication. Maintenance reliability. Revenue performance. Transition quality. Guest experience standards.

Pick one. Lead with it in the opening exchange. Return to it at the end.

Script: "We've covered a lot of ground today. The thing I'd want you to come back to when you're comparing your options is how we handle communication. I know that's where operators lose trust fast, and it's the thing we've invested the most in getting right. If you want to know how that actually works day to day, that's the conversation I'd want to have before you make a decision."

The owner who leaves thinking about owner communication will evaluate the contract, the follow-up call, and the comparison with other operators through that lens.

Field note: Controller owners require a specific focal dimension: control. Lead with how they stay informed, what decisions remain theirs, and how the communication structure works in practice. If you lead with revenue or guest experience, you're competing on dimensions the Controller doesn't weight heavily.
