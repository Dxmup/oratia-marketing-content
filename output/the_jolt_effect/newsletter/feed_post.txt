You are not losing most of your stalled deals to the manager down the street. You are losing them to no decision at all.

Dixon and McKenna's research finding is one the VRM industry hasn't fully internalized: 40 to 60% of lost deals end with the owner doing nothing. Not switching to a competitor. Staying put. The owner who had a great appointment and then went cold isn't still evaluating. They've run a worst-case scenario in their head about what happens if the switch doesn't work out, and the status quo feels safer than finding out.

More information doesn't fix this. Risk removal does. The newsletter this week covers the FOMU psychology in detail, how to diagnose whether a stalled deal is FOMU or competitive, and how to engineer your offer so the switch costs the owner less than staying where they are. The full transition friction stack is in there: every barrier between a verbal yes and a live property, and what to do about each one. There's also the cost-of-inaction frame, which is the most counterintuitive tool in the JOLT framework and the one that moves the most deals.
