THE ACCUSATION AUDIT ISN'T ONE SCRIPT. IT'S SIX.

The generic Accusation Audit, "you're probably expecting an inflated projection and then radio silence," only fully lands with one or two owner types. The other four have different fears, and a fear you don't name doesn't get neutralized.

Voss's insight is that naming a negative emotion defuses it. The mechanism is the same across owner types. The application has to be calibrated to what each type actually fears.

THE SKEPTIC: Has been burned. Name the personal history of failure, not just the industry pattern.

Script: "I'm guessing at some point you had an experience with a manager that didn't go the way it was described at signing. I'd rather tell you where managers typically fail and let you tell me whether any of those match what you experienced."

THE STEWARD: Fears no one will care about the property the way they do.

Script: "Handing the keys to a property like this to someone you just met is a real act of trust. The fear that whoever takes over won't care about it the way you do is completely legitimate. I'd rather put that on the table now."

THE INVESTOR: Afraid of being oversold and underdelivered.

Script: "Many managers show you the best-case scenario and call it a projection. I'm going to show you three scenarios today and tell you which one I think is realistic. If that number doesn't work for your investment thesis, you should know that now."

THE DELEGATOR: Afraid that "hands-off" requires more of their hands than advertised.

Script: "Some owners come expecting zero involvement and find out there are two or three moments a year where we need a decision from them. Here's what those moments look like and how often they actually happen."

THE CONTROLLER: Afraid of losing oversight over something they've managed carefully.

Script: "I want to show you specifically what stays with you and what transfers to us, so there's no ambiguity about that later."

THE OPPORTUNIST: Commitment anxiety, not hostile fear. Name the absence of pressure.

Script: "There's no pressure here. I'm not going to ask you to sign anything today. My goal is to give you accurate information so you can make a good decision, whatever that turns out to be."

Field note: Run the audit before any data, before any fee conversation, before anything that could activate resistance. The audit that opens the appointment creates the conditions for everything that follows.
