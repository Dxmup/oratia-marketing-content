YOUR PITCH STOPPED WORKING. THE MOST LIKELY REASON IS NOT THAT IT WAS EVER WRONG.

Eugene Schwartz wrote Breakthrough Advertising in 1966. It is one of the most important books on persuasion ever written. It contains one concept that explains a problem many VRM operators don't know they have.

Schwartz called it market sophistication.

The idea: every market moves through predictable stages as buyers become more familiar with the available options and the available claims. In an early market, a direct claim is enough. By the time a market matures, that same claim is noise.

Here is what this means for VRM acquisition.

THE STANDARD PITCH IS PROBABLY NOISE IN YOUR MARKET

In most markets of any size, property owners have heard the same pitch from multiple managers before they talk to you. They've heard "we maximize your revenue," "we treat your home like our own," and "our team is local and responsive" from at least three competitors.

When they hear it from you, they are not evaluating it as a fresh claim. They are categorizing it as one more version of what everyone says.

Your pitch may be accurate. Your service may be better. But if your communication is pitched at the same level as your competitors', you will be heard as one more version of the same thing.

WHERE MOST VRM MARKETS ARE RIGHT NOW

Schwartz described five stages. By stage 3, the market has heard the claim and is no longer moved by it. They need the mechanism: the specific, credible explanation of why the result is achievable and how this operator produces it.

Most established VRM markets are at stage 3 or approaching stage 4. Owners have heard the claim. They don't believe it or disbelieve it. They've just heard it too many times to respond.

THE UPGRADE

Stage 3 claim: "We maximize your revenue."

Stage 3 mechanism: "We run a pricing model calibrated against comparable properties within a two-mile radius, updated every 24 hours. That practice is why our owners average 18 percent more revenue than comparable listings on the same platforms."

Same claim. The mechanism makes it credible, specific, and different. A competitor who also claims revenue maximization doesn't automatically undercut a mechanism. They have to counter it.

The first step is diagnosing which stage your market has reached. The second is upgrading the pitch to match.
