THE LISTING APPOINTMENT IS A PREVIEW, NOT A PITCH

Most VRM operators think the listing appointment is where they explain their service.

The owner is deciding something different. They are trying to figure out what working with this company is going to feel like.

David Priemer's core argument in Sell the Way You Buy is that the buying experience predicts the product. How the sales process feels is evidence of what the service relationship will feel like. Owners use this information even when they don't consciously realize they're using it.

A listing appointment that is disorganized says something. One that is dominated by the operator talking says something. One where the operator is checking boxes rather than listening to the specific concerns the owner raised says something. The owner may not be able to articulate what bothered them, but they registered it.

WHAT AN APPOINTMENT-AS-PREVIEW LOOKS LIKE

The pre-meeting communication. Does the operator confirm the meeting with a clear agenda and any materials the owner should review? Or does the operator just show up? The pre-meeting communication is part of the preview.

The opening of the meeting. Does the operator start with a question about the owner's situation, or do they open their laptop and start a presentation? The opening is part of the preview.

The ratio of listening to talking. An operator who talks 80% of the time during the listing appointment is telling the owner something about how they will respond to owner communication for the next 12 months.

The post-meeting follow-up. Does the operator follow up with something specific that responds to the conversation, or does the operator send a generic thank-you note and a proposal? The follow-up is part of the preview.

Field note: Controller and Skeptic owners are most sensitive to how the appointment is structured. They pay close attention to whether the operator adapts to their questions or pushes through their own agenda. The preview matters more with them than with any other owner type.
