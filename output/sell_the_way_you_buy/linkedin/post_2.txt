THE REVENUE PROJECTION DOES NOT WIN THE LISTING APPOINTMENT

Owners make their decision emotionally before they make it rationally.

The revenue projection, the platform tour, the dynamic pricing explanation: these confirm a decision that was already forming in the first few minutes of the meeting. If the emotional register was off at the start, the numbers do not fix it.

Priemer draws on behavioral science to make a point most sales trainers avoid: buyers process emotionally first and rationalize afterward. The logical case is used to justify the emotional conclusion, not to drive it.

For VRM operators, this means the listing appointment is won or lost before the presentation starts.

THE FIRST FIFTEEN MINUTES

Most operators walk into a listing appointment and open with an overview. Market data, management structure, fee schedule, platform capabilities. They are ready to present.

The owner is not ready to evaluate. They are still figuring out whether this person understands them.

A different approach for the first fifteen minutes:

Ask about their current situation without an agenda. Not "what are your pain points" as a funnel for your solution. Genuine curiosity about what they've tried, what has worked, and what hasn't.

Script: "Before I show you anything about how we work, I want to understand your property and where you're coming from. Can you walk me through how things are currently managed and what prompted you to explore a change?"

Then stay in it. Ask follow-up questions. Let the owner talk for ten to twelve minutes before saying anything about your service.

What happens in that window is the emotional decision. The owner either feels understood or they don't. If they feel understood, the presentation confirms what they already felt. If they don't, no presentation recovers it.

Field note: Steward and Investor owner types have different reasons for feeling understood. The Steward needs to believe you see the property the way they do. The Investor needs to believe you see the return potential they see. Ask different questions for each.
