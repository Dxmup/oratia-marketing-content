Owners who ghost after a good appointment aren't confused about pricing. They hit a certainty gap in the operator, the system, or the brand, and no one in the room noticed which one.

Jordan Belfort's Way of the Wolf is the framework I keep coming back to when I see it. Not because it's a VRM book. Because it's the most precise map I've found for what's actually happening in that room.

This week I'm running three posts applying it to owner acquisition. If you work in VRM business development, these are worth your time.
