WHEN THE CHALLENGER APPROACH CREATES FRICTION INSTEAD OF AUTHORITY

The Challenger Sale's research was built on B2B data. The framework works well with Investor and Skeptic owners. It creates friction with two types where the sequence needs adjustment before the insight can land.

This isn't a failure of the framework. It's a calibration question.

THE STEWARD

The Steward owns the property emotionally. When you open with a market analysis showing how much it's underperforming, you've led with criticism of something they love, and they register it that way even when the data is accurate.

The Steward's primary fear isn't underperformance. It's that whoever takes over won't care about the property the way they do. Walking in as a data-first authority figure deepens that fear.

With a Steward, invert the sequence. Listen first. Ask about the property's story. Let them walk you through what they're proud of. Then introduce the insight as a response to their story.

Script: "What you've described, the repeat bookings from families who come back every summer, that's something specific you've built here. I'd like to show you how that positioning compares to your comp set, because I think there's a meaningful opportunity your current setup is missing. Can I show you?"

The insight lands completely differently as a response to their story than as a prepared opening.

THE OPPORTUNIST

The Opportunist hasn't fully committed to renting. Opening with a gap analysis implies a level of commitment they haven't made yet. You're solving a problem they haven't decided they have.

With an Opportunist, lead with opportunity, not the gap.

Script: "I pulled some data on properties similar to yours in this market. I'm not going to tell you what to do with it, but the numbers might be useful if you're still figuring out whether this makes sense for you. Want to take a look?"

THE UNDERLYING RULE

Disruption only registers when the person can receive it. Skeptics and Investors are already there. Stewards need emotional credibility first. Opportunists need pressure removed.

Same insight. Different sequence. Different result.

Field note: Useful pre-appointment signals for a Steward: property that's been in the family for decades, a listing with the same cover photo from three years ago, an owner profile showing personal involvement in reviews. Earn the right to teach before you open with the lesson.
