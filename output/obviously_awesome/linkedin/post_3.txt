YOUR FEATURE LIST IS NOT YOUR PITCH. IT'S YOUR EVIDENCE.

Dynamic pricing. Professional photography. A 24/7 maintenance line. A dedicated owner portal.

If you have been in more than ten listing appointments, you know these are on every manager's list. Operators keep leading with them anyway, as if reciting the same features more confidently will produce a different result.

April Dunford calls this the feature-first failure. It is common in VRM acquisition. Features without a positioning frame do not differentiate. They invite comparison. When the owner can't tell you apart from the last two managers they interviewed, the problem usually started in the first sixty seconds of the pitch.

THE REORDER THAT CHANGES THE APPOINTMENT

The fix is not new features. It's the sequence.

Position first. Features second.

Position means: one clear sentence on who you are specifically for and what outcome they get. Not a slogan. A frame the owner can use to figure out whether you're the right fit.

Before: "We offer dynamic pricing, professional photography, owner communication within four hours, and a 24/7 maintenance coordination line."

Owner's response: sounds like everyone else, what's the commission?

After: "We built our operation for owners who want a property that runs itself. They hear from us when something needs a decision. Otherwise, it runs. Everything we do is designed around that outcome."

Then the features follow as examples of how that outcome is delivered.

Owner's response: is this what I actually want? Do I fit here?

That is a qualification conversation, not a comparison conversation.

WHAT THE REORDER REQUIRES

Knowing who you are specifically for means being willing to name it. Many operators resist because it feels like narrowing. It's not. It's filtering. The wrong owners disqualify themselves earlier. The right owners recognize themselves immediately.

Script: "Before I walk you through how we work, let me describe the type of owner we're built for, so you can tell me if that sounds like you." Then describe the profile specifically. The owner either nods or tells you something useful.

Field note: This opener works particularly well with Delegator and Investor types who came ready to hand over the keys if the right fit exists. They are not shopping features. The positioning-first approach gives them permission to stop comparing and start qualifying.

At Oratia, the appointments that stall at commission objections are almost always the ones that led with features before establishing a frame.
