THE COMPETITOR IN MOST OF YOUR DEALS ISN'T ANOTHER MANAGER

For many of the owners in your pipeline, you are not competing against the company that pitched them on Tuesday.

You are competing against what they are already doing.

April Dunford's positioning framework starts with a simple question: what would the customer do if your product didn't exist? She calls this the competitive alternative. It is not always what operators assume.

Many VRM operators spend their competitive energy on other management companies. They prepare to differentiate on commission rate, software, review scores. They walk into listing appointments ready to explain why they're better than the last two managers the owner spoke to.

But for a portion of prospective owners, the comparison isn't between management companies at all. It's between professional management and what they're currently doing: some version of self-management, a long-term rental, or nothing organized.

THE PITCH THAT BEATS SELF-MANAGEMENT IS DIFFERENT

When the real alternative is self-management, the entire frame shifts.

The conversation isn't: here's why we're better than the other managers you talked to.

It's: here's what you are actually paying to keep doing what you're doing.

That means pricing the owner's time. Calculating what a bad guest experience costs when there's no professional between them and a 2 AM lockout call. Showing what calendar gaps look like when no one is actively managing distribution.

Script: "I want to understand your current situation before I describe how we work. How much time are you spending on the property each week, including guest communication, maintenance coordination, and cleaning oversight? And how do the summers go when you're managing occupancy yourself?"

That question sequence changes the appointment. The owner is now calculating their own current costs, not comparing your feature list to the last manager's.

WHAT CHANGES WHEN YOU GET THIS RIGHT

When you correctly identify the real competitive alternative, your pitch becomes more specific and more honest. You stop competing on dimensions that aren't relevant to half your prospects.

Field note: This matters most with first-time rental owners, Investor types who bought a vacation property as a business decision and haven't tried professional management before. The Skeptic who has cycled through two prior managers is a different conversation. Know which one you're in before you pitch.
