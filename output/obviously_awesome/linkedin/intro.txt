VRM Sales Library | Obviously Awesome | April Dunford
Many VRM operators think differentiation happens in the listing appointment. That framing makes it harder than it needs to be.

April Dunford's Obviously Awesome reframes it. The owner has already started positioning you before you walk in. If you didn't design that position deliberately, they used the default: property manager, a commodity category with commodity evaluation criteria.

Three posts this week working through the application. Worth following if owner acquisition is part of your job.
