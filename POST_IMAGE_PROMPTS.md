# LinkedIn Post Image Prompts
# VRM Sales Library — Post Visual Assets

## Overview

Not every post needs a visual. The posts selected here have clear framework structures that reward visualization: numbered systems, comparison tables, owner-type grids, and before/after sequences. Narrative posts and script-heavy posts are left as text — the intimacy of those pieces lives in the prose.

Visuals are ranked by expected impact:
- **★★★ High** — Reference-quality content; practitioners will save and reshare
- **★★ Medium** — Clear framework that benefits from visual structure
- **★ Lower** — Helpful but not essential

---

## Visual Style Guide (Post Images)

All post images follow the established brand palette:

- **Background options:** Deep navy `#1A2B45` (primary) or warm cream `#F5F0E8` (lighter posts)
- **Text:** White on navy, near-black (`#1A1A1A`) on cream
- **Accent / highlight color:** Warm amber `#C8843A`
- **Font:** Montserrat Bold (headers), Montserrat Regular (body), ALL CAPS for section labels
- **Watermark:** `VRM SALES LIBRARY` in small amber uppercase, bottom-right corner
- **Format:** 1200×1200px square (LinkedIn feed optimal)
- **Style:** Clean information design — no decorative elements that don't carry content. No stock photography. No gradients except subtle depth treatments on dark backgrounds.

**Production note:** For text-heavy infographics (tables, grids), generate the background layout and structural elements with AI, then add all text in Canva using Montserrat. AI image generators are unreliable with precise multi-column text. The prompts below mark where this applies.
